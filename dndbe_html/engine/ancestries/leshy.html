Leshy

A Leshy is "born" when a skilled druid or other master of primal magic conducts a ritual to create a suitable vessel, and then a spirit chooses that vessel to be their temporary home. Leshys are self-sufficient from the moment the ritual ends, and so they do not depend upon these druids for care, though it is not uncommon for a leshy to maintain lifelong bonds with their creators.

Not all leshys have a strong enough spirit to strike off completely on their own, however. Weaker nature spirits can form only tenuous bonds that are just strong enough to animate Tiny bodies. These spirits become leshy familiars, acting as companions to their druid creators. Independent leshys are often protective of such leshy familiars, advocating for them to be treated with dignity and respect.

Leshys are as varied as the material used to create their vessels, usually appearing as a bizarre mishmash of various plants or fungi, sometimes entangling the remains of one or more animals. The druids who create them favor using various fungi, gourds, leaves, and vines, but occasionally use fruits, cactus, or other plant material. Their bodies are most often vaguely humanoid in shape, though the ritual requires Large leshys to be quadrapedal - in the shape of a deer, horse, moose or similar.

Due to the nature of the ritual used to create a leshy, they begin their lives as adults. As spirits, they do not age and a leshy could potentially remain in the same vessel forever, though they would rarely consider that outcome to be desireable.

Leshys of Many Worlds

On any given world in the multiverse, the material culture of humans can change from one region to another. In the Forgotten Realms, for example, the clothing, architecture, cuisine, music, and literature are wildly different in the Western Heartlands than in the frozen north of Icewind Dale.

As humans migrate, they bring elements of their cultures with them, giving rise to diverse settlements that contain people with different physical characteristics, not to mention a medley of clothing styles, customs, and beliefs.
